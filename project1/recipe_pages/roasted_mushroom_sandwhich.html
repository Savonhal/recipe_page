Roasted Mushroom Sandwich with Horseradish Aioli

Layers of roasty mushrooms, havarti, lemony arugula, and an easy homemade horseradish aioli make this mushroom sandwich a WINNER. A saucy, salty, hefty masterpiece!



Flavor Explosion

Indulge in the rich, earthy flavors of roasted mushrooms perfectly complemented by the zesty kick of horseradish aioli in this delectable sandwich. This recipe combines tender roasted mushrooms with a creamy, tangy horseradish-infused aioli, all sandwiched between slices of hearty bread for a satisfying meal bursting with flavor. Whether you're a dedicated mushroom lover or simply seeking a delicious vegetarian option, this roasted mushroom sandwich is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. Let's dive into the recipe and elevate your sandwich game!

Why this Dish?

Earthiness meets zing in this Roasted Mushroom Sandwich with Horseradish Aioli – a flavor explosion between two slices of bread!

INSTRUCTIONS

Roast the mushrooms: Preheat the oven to 450 degrees. Toss the sliced mushrooms with the avocado oil and the seasoning on a sheet pan until well-combined. Roast for 20-25 minutes, until golden brown.

Make the horseradish aioli: Mix all the sauce ingredients in a bowl until well-combined. Taste and season.

Assemble the sandwiches:Toast the bread for the last few minutes on the pan with the mushrooms. Split the bread, spread with pesto rosso, roasty mushrooms, havarti (pop it in the oven again for a minute if you want it to melt), arugula, and the top piece of bread shmeared with the horseradish sauce

Ingredients

Roasted Mushrooms

16 ounces sliced mushrooms (portobello or white button mushrooms)
2 tablespoons avocado oil
2 tablespoons Cajun seasoning (if it has salt in it, you may want to add less of it just to make sure the mushrooms don’t get too salty – every brand is different)
salt and pepper to taste
1/2 cup mayo

Horseradish Aioli

1 small clove garlic, grated (even half a clove is plenty)
1–2 teaspoons horseradish sauce, to taste (I use Boar’s Head)
1–2 teaspoons lemon juice, to taste
salt, to taste

Sandwhiches

Big honking chunk of bread that you really love
Pesto rosso (optional as a second sauce)
Havarti cheese slices
Arugula tossed with olive oil and lemon
